One of my favorite parts of golf is watching tournaments. I like seeing how professionals handle the same challenges I face on the course, only with so much more control and focus. Golf tournaments show just how mental the sport is, as players can be tied for hours, and one bad swing or missed putt can change everything. My favorite is the Ryder Cup because it’s different from the others. Instead of players competing for themselves, it’s about playing your team and country. The energy feels higher, and every shot matters not just for one person, but for everyone on the team. I also like the team formats like foursomes and four-ball because they make more important. You see players adjusting their game to fit with their partner’s strengths. The crowds are louder, the celebrations are bigger, and the pressure feels more real. Watching the Ryder Cup makes me appreciate how golf can be both an individual and a team sport at the same time. It’s one of the few times in golf where emotion takes over, and I think that’s what makes it so exciting to watch.
